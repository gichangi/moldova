National Child Protection Program 2022- 2026 Action Plan Monitoring Tool Guidelines
Introduction & Purpose
The purpose of the National Child Protection Program Monitoring Tool is to track progress towards achievement of the 2022- 2026 Action Plan. The tool details 3 general objectives, 11 specific objectives and 4 cross cutting actions all of which have well defined indicators, baselines and targets. The tool is designed to track the yearly progress and overall achievements. It includes 4 data entry tabs detailed below for each General Objective and a Scoreboard.  
   GO1 action plan monitoring tool is a matrix designed to track progress towards strengthening of the Moldova Child Protection System to efficiently address the needs of every child while increasing the share of resources allocated to child and family protection. The matrix includes 4 specific objectives,  32 well-defined actions with indicators, baselines and targets.
   GO2 action plan monitoring tool is  designed to track progress towards ensuring adults and children have zero tolerance for any form of violence against children. The tool has 3 specific objectives, 11 actions with indicators baselines and targets.
  GO3 action plan monitoring tool is to track progress towards ensuring children grow up in a secure and protective family environment which guarantees their well-being and reduction of absolute poverty rate of households with 3 and more children. The tab has 5 specific objectives, 25 actions with indicators baselines and targets.
  Cross cutting action plan monitoring tool is designed to track progress towards strengthening National Council for Child Rights Protection. The tab has 4 actions each with indicators baselines and targets.
   Dashboard The tool also includes a dashboard that provides at-a-glance view of key performance indicators . This is to gain insight into the implementation progress and performance with regards to child protection in Moldova. The dashboard updates realtime with data from the 4 matrices.
The four matrices and dashboard are designed to track progress and performance over the 5-year period and calculate achievement for each specific objective and the overall achievements providing users with a status report on what is lagging behind to enable informed decision making.
Data Entry Guidelines
The three general objectives and cross cutting actions all have overall baselines and targets  for each indicator which are then unpacked for each year of implementation depending on the indicator. The targets and achievements could be annual or cumulative and are used to generate the annual performance and the overall performance for the five-year period
Measures:The tool makes use of different types of measures for each indicator to enable collection of quantitative and qualitative data for instance a) numerical (e.g. number of trainings, number of children) b) Likert scales (e.g. 0-Not Approved ,1-Approved) c) proportion (%) and d) ratios.
To ensure uniformity and ability to aggregate scores, the different measures are converted into percentages to generate the overall percentage achieved against target for each indicator which are then used to generate the specific objective scores and the general objective scores.
Targets: The tool has two categories of targets ie 5-year targets and annual targets for each of the years. The targets can be annual or cummulative depending on type and this is clearly indicated for each cell with a target to guide the user.
Achievements: Achievements should be documented per year and there are formulars to calculate the overall 5 year achievements from the yearly achievements statistics. Achievements are documented as per the guide below depending on the type of indicator and measures.
Type of Measure  Required Response 
Numerical: Number of trainings…, Number of children…, Number of studies conducted … Respond with the actual number of persons reached, events held etc….
Proportion (%): Share of children….,Share of young people…, Share of employees…, Respond with the  percentage/proportion of persons reached, events held etc….
2-point Likert Scale 0,1: 0 (Not done), 1 (Yes Done).  Respond  with 1 if activity is fully done or 0 if activity/event is not done 
5-point Likert Scale 1,2,3,4,5: 1) No action has been taken, 2) Action initiated 3) Action progressed 4)Action advanced 5)Fully implemented Respond with the actual status of the activity along the 5-point likert scale 
Scoreboard
Moldova National Child Protection Program 2022-2026 Implementation Progress & Performance Scoreboard is a dashboard built to provide quick insights showing progress towards the set goals for child protection on Moldova. Using the scoreboard, one is able to see the relationship between performance of the general objectives, specific objectives and relevant actions.
The scoreboard is organized into two chapters as detailed below;
1. Chapter 1: Overall progress monitoring, General objective progress monitoring and Specific Objective progress monitoring.
2. Chapter 2: Indicator performance monitoring against targets for both General and Specific Objectives
Chapter 1: Overall progress monitoring, General objective progress monitoring and Specific Objective progress monitoring.
Overall Implementation Progress score is generated from a calculation of the average progress scores of the three general objectives and cross cutting actions these scores are generated in column X of the respective matrices
General objectives progress scores are generated by calculating an average of the specific objective indicators performance scores for each respective general objective for example GO1 progress score is generated by averaging scores of the 4 sub-objectves progress scores in column X
Cross cutting progress score is generated by averaging the progress scores of the four cross cutting actions for example crosscutting progress score is generated by averaging scores of the 4 actions related to cross cutting which is X4 to X10
Specific objective progress scores are generated by averaging the progress scores of actions under each specific objective for example the progress score for SO1.1 is generated by averaging scores of the 32 actions related to SO1.1 which is X9 to X32
Chapter 2: Indicator performance monitoring against targets for both General and Specific Objectives
General objectives performance monitoring: Focus is on the three impact indicators performance and a comparison of the achievement against the set targets over the five-year period. This is done to generate the three charts for each impact indicator
Specific objectives performance monitoring: Focus is on respective indicators for each specific objectives performance and a comparison of the achievement against the set targets for each indicator over the five-year period. In a case where a specific objective has two indicators two charts are drawn to show performance of each indicator. The performance for each year is also compared to the overall performance of the specific indicator by a comparison of the overall set target and average performance.
